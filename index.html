State-Space Life Model

Life as a trajectory — not a clock

🧠 The Core Idea

Instead of measuring life by time passing, this model defines life as the total distance a system travels through state space. A rich life = a long exploratory path. A constrained life = a short, collapsing one.

"Life is not how long you exist — it's how much of the possible space you explore."

📐 The Equations

X = (E, I, S, C)State vector: Energy, Information, Entropy, Constraint
X(t+1) = F(Xt, Ut, Kt)Next state = current state + decisions + constraints
L = ∫ ||dX||Life = total accumulated path length through state space

📊 The 4 Dimensions

X AXIS
E — Energy

Available energy. Peaks at life's peak, collapses in decline.

Y AXIS
I — Information

Complexity and memory. Grows through life, slows in decline.

Z AXIS
S — Entropy

Disorder. Always rising. Resistance fights it — until decline.

HIDDEN
C — Constraint

Environment shaping which transitions are possible.

🎛 Parameters (Real Life Meaning)

🌍 Life HardshipHow difficult your environment is. High = fewer choices, smaller steps allowed.
⚡ Resources & WealthAccess to energy, money, health. Caps the maximum E dimension.
🧠 Curiosity & LearningHow fast information (I) grows each step. Drives vertical rise.
💪 Will to LiveEntropy resistance. Keeps you in Peak longer. Core "life force".
🔒 Social ConstraintsRules and culture limiting your path choices.
🎲 Risk TakingSelection randomness. High = unpredictable adventurous life.
🔥 Ambition & DriveEnergy flow per step. Bold vs quiet life trajectory.
🚀 Life Change RateStep size. Small routines vs major life leaps.

🔁 Rebirth

When death completes, the particle dissolves and a new generation begins with slight variation. Best L tracks which life explored the most state space across all generations.

💡 The Central Insight

A person with a short calendar life but high curiosity, strong will, and rich experiences can have a longer L than someone who lived many passive years. Time is not life. The path is.
