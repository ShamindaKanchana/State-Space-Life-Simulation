State-Space Life Model

Life as a trajectory — not a clock

🧠 The Core Idea

Instead of measuring life by time passing, this model defines life as the total distance a system travels through state space. A rich, complex life = a long exploratory path. A constrained life = a short, collapsing one.

📐 The Equations

X = (E, I, S, C)State vector: Energy, Information, Entropy, Constraint
X(t+1) = F(Xt, Ut, Kt)Next state = function of current state + decisions + constraints
L = ∫ ||dX||Life = total accumulated path length through state space

🌱 The 5 Life Phases

PhaseAge %Ball ColorWhat Happens
Birth0–10%■ BlueSmall, fragile. Energy builds slowly. Entropy very low. State space exploration just beginning.
Growth10–40%■ Cyan-GreenEnergy rising, information accumulating fast. Ball grows. Transitions become bolder and wider.
Peak40–60%■ GoldMaximum vitality. Largest ball, brightest glow. Energy and information at their highest. Entropy resistance strongest.
Decline60–85%■ OrangeEnergy availability drops. Transitions shrink. Entropy resistance weakens. Path starts collapsing inward.
Death85–100%■ GreySystem loses coherence. Ball shrinks and dims. Path freezes. Then: rebirth of a new generation begins.

📊 The 4 State Dimensions

X AXIS — RED
E — Energy

Available energy. High E = bold transitions. Low E = trapped. Peaks at life's peak phase, collapses in decline.

Y AXIS — GREEN
I — Information

Complexity, memory, structure. Grows through life, slows in decline. The vertical rise of the trajectory.

Z AXIS — BLUE
S — Entropy

Disorder. Always rising. Entropy resistance fights it — but in decline, the fight weakens and entropy wins.

HIDDEN
C — Constraint

Environmental interaction. Shapes which transitions are possible. Tightens as the system ages and weakens.

🎛 Sliders Reference

LifespanHow many state transitions constitute one full life. More steps = slower aging, longer exploration.
Environment PressureRestricts reachable next states. High = trapped in small region.
Energy AvailabilityCaps maximum E. Reduced automatically during Decline phase.
Information GrowthHow fast I axis increases each step. Boosts trajectory height.
Entropy ResistanceFights upward S drift. Weakens during Decline. This is the core "life force" parameter.
Exploration RandomnessTemperature of state selection. High = creative/risky. Low = conservative/optimal.
Step SizePhysical scale of each transition. Automatically shrinks in Decline and Death phases.

🔁 Rebirth

When entropy overwhelms the system (Death phase ends), the particle dissolves and a new generation begins at the origin. Each generation inherits a slightly different starting state — simulating how life learns from previous cycles. The generation counter and best trajectory score track across all lives.
